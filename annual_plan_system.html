On-Site Upsell — After Completing a Job
Use this when you're packing up and the homeowner comes out to look at the results. This is your highest-conversion moment — they're happy, they've seen your work.
⏱ Timing: Start this conversation right when they're admiring the result. They're emotionally engaged — this is not the time to talk price first.
Open — compliment, then plant the seed "Looks great, right? The difference is always bigger than people expect. The thing is — with Florida's humidity, you'll see that algae start coming back within 18 to 24 months. It's just how it is down here, especially with all the oak canopy in this neighborhood." Transition — make them feel taken care of "A lot of my customers in [Blue Lake Estates / Park Place] just put themselves on my annual plan so they don't have to think about it. I contact them when it's time, they're always first on the schedule, and they save $100–250 compared to booking one-off each year." Offer — specific, not vague "For a home like yours — roof plus driveway — we're looking at the Complete Plan for $749 a year. That's basically locking in today's price, getting priority scheduling, and making sure this never gets as bad as it was before I showed up." Close — give them a choice, not a yes/no "Do you want to pay annually or would you prefer I split it into two payments of $375?"
Common Objection
"Let me think about it / I'll call you when I need it."
"Totally understand. One thing I'd mention — I'm usually booked 3–4 weeks out in the busy season, and plan customers always get first access. But no pressure — if you want to save the plan info and decide later, I'll leave you this." [Hand them the leave-behind.]
Common Objection
"I just paid you — I don't want to pay again already."
"That's fair — and actually you wouldn't pay anything today. The annual plan kicks in a year from now when it's time for the next clean. You're just locking in the price and the spot."

Phone / Text Pitch — When Someone Calls for a Quote
Use this when a new lead contacts you. They haven't seen your work yet, so lead with the value before you lead with price.
Answer the question, then plant the seed "For a house your size — roof soft wash runs about $X to $X, driveway's usually around $X. Most customers bundle them the same day and save on mobilization.

One thing worth mentioning — I do have an annual maintenance plan that a lot of Lake County homeowners use. It locks in your pricing, you get priority scheduling, and you save about $100–150 a year compared to booking one-off. I can send you the details when I drop the quote — just let you know the option exists." Get the appointment — don't close the plan on the phone "Let me come take a look and I'll get you exact numbers. What's the address?"

Referral Ask — After Selling an Annual Plan
Someone just signed up. They're your happiest customer in this moment. Ask immediately.
"One more thing — I'm currently focused on building up clients in [Blue Lake Estates / Park Place / this neighborhood] so I can stay close to home and keep turnaround times tight.

If you have a neighbor or two whose roof or driveway has been bugging you — or them — I'd love an introduction. I'll give them $50 off their first job, and I'll credit you $50 on your next renewal. Most of my best customers came from one good referral."
